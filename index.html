AI Safety Researcher

Diving deep into the foundations of AI safety and alignment. Currently exploring how we can make AI systems more interpretable, controllable, and aligned with human values.

Current Focus

My research centers on mechanistic interpretability and AI safety evaluation. I'm particularly interested in understanding how language models represent and process information internally, and how we can use this understanding to build safer systems.

Key areas of exploration include causal intervention methods, activation steering, and developing robust evaluation protocols for detecting alignment failures before they manifest in model outputs.

Research Philosophy

True AI safety requires understanding what's happening inside the black box. I approach this through a combination of empirical investigation and theoretical frameworks, always asking: "What would we need to know to be confident this system is safe?"

  • Transparency First: Build systems we can actually understand and verify
  • Empirical Rigor: Test safety claims with concrete experiments and measurements
  • Practical Impact: Bridge the gap between research insights and deployable solutions
